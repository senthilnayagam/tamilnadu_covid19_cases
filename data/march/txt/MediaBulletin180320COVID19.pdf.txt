     State Control Room, Directorate of Public Health and Preventive Medicine
     Health and Family Welfare Department, Government of Tamil Nadu
                           Media Bulletin 18.03.2020
                 Daily report on Public Health measures taken for
                    COVID-19 Infection prevention and control
      WHO has declared COVID-19 affecting 159 countries as Pandemic. In this
context, Government of Tamil Nadu has strengthened the surveillance and control
measures against the disease, as per the national guidelines.

       All the passengers from all the international flights are screened universally
through thermal screening. All over India the screening is carried out in 30 airports.
In Tamil Nadu, till date 189750 passengers are screened at the Airports of Chennai,
Trichy, Madurai, and Coimbatore. 2984 passengers are under home quarantine for 28
days and 32 are under hospital isolation.

       Till now 291 samples have been taken from the passengers and sent for testing
at King Institute of Preventive Medicine & Research, Guindy Chennai, National
Institute of Virology, Pune, Theni VRDL, Thiruvarur VRDL and Tirunelveli VRDL. The
samples of 234 passengers are processed of which 232 samples are negative, 2
samples are positive for COVID-19 infection, 56 samples are under process and 1
sample in transit.

       A previously COVID-19 infection positive passenger was discharged after two
consecutive samples tested negative from RGGGH, Chennai. A 20 years old passenger
with travel history to Delhi who tested positive for COVID-19 is stable and under
observation in RGGGH, Chennai. He will be discharged after two consecutive samples
tested negative.

      In the background of the present COVID-19 pandemic, the general public are
advised to adhere to health advisories and travel advisories issued by the state
Government. Public should follow the cough etiquette by covering the face using
handkerchief / towel while sneezing / coughing. Do frequent hand-washing with soap
and water.

        People are advised to refrain from travelling to China, Hong Kong, Republic of
Korea, Italy, Iran, France, Spain & Germany and advised to avoid non- essential travel
to other COVID-19 affected countries. All people coming from China, Hong Kong,
Republic of Korea, Italy, Iran, France, Spain, UAE, Qatar, Oman, Kuwait & Germany
or having such travel history should remain under strict home quarantine or hospital
isolation for 28 days from the date of arrival in India. Further travel of passengers
from members countries of the European union, Turkey, United Kingdom,
Afghanistan, Philippines and Malaysia to India is prohibited with immediate effect
temporarily till 31st March 2020.
